# Can DAOs Work Together?

## The Plain English Version

### The problem

DAOs don't exist in a vacuum. They share users, face common problems (security, infrastructure, regulation), and could accomplish a lot more by cooperating. But most attempts at inter-DAO collaboration fail. Why?

### What we did

We simulated 150 multi-DAO scenarios with different setups:
- **Homogeneous** -- three similar DAOs trying to cooperate
- **Heterogeneous** -- DAOs with different governance rules working together
- **Asymmetric** -- a large, wealthy DAO partnering with smaller ones
- **Specialized** -- DAOs with different focuses (development, investing, community)
- **Isolated** -- a control group with no cooperation at all

### What we found

**The cooperation paradox: the partnerships worth having are the hardest to form.**

Similar DAOs agree easily but don't gain much from working together -- they're too alike to bring new value. Diverse DAOs bring complementary strengths (one has money, another has developers, a third has community) and could create real value together. But their differences make it hard to agree on anything.

**Size mismatches kill partnerships without fairness rules.** When a large DAO partners with a small one, the small DAO tends to get a raw deal unless there are explicit rules about how to split costs and benefits. Simple 50/50 splits don't work because the contributions aren't equal. You need proportional allocation based on what each party actually brings.

**Shared members are the secret ingredient.** When people hold tokens in multiple DAOs, they naturally align incentives across organizations. Our simulations showed that 20% membership overlap roughly doubles the success rate of cooperative proposals. These cross-DAO members act as bridges, translating between different community cultures.

**Cooperation success rates are still low overall.** Even in the best configurations, inter-DAO proposals had success rates around 15-28%. This is much better than the isolated baseline (0%), but it shows that cross-organization governance is fundamentally harder than single-DAO decision making.

### The bottom line

- Seek partners that complement your strengths, not duplicate them
- Build trust through small collaborations before attempting big ones
- Use explicit, transparent rules for dividing costs and benefits
- Encourage members to participate in partner DAOs
- For three or more DAOs, designate a coordinator role (but rotate it)
- Accept that inter-DAO cooperation is hard and plan for a lower success rate than internal governance

**The big picture:** The DAO ecosystem faces problems no single organization can solve alone -- public goods funding, protocol interoperability, shared security standards. Cooperation is achievable, but it requires intentional design, not just good intentions.
